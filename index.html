I am a clinician with over 12 years experience in the NHS and private sector. My main areas of interest are strategic planning and innovation with a particular emphasis on remote consultation and collaborative multi-disciplinary working. I am passionate about improving quality of healthcare both from a clinician and patient perspective by developing smart, efficient working strategies and utilising the latest in point of care testing, near patient monitoring and machine learning. In addition to clinical skills in primary care (including minor skin surgery) and emergency medicine, I am proficient in computer programming in Python and have basic HTML skills. I am well versed in the main GP patient management systems, Vision, EMIS, Adastra, Heydoc. Drawing the skills that I have developed in my career from quick, high pressured, decison making as a registrar in head and neck surgery, to calculated, investigative report writing as a registrar in pathology, I am able to adapt to the tasks at hand.

Clinical Leadership & Management

Clinical Director of Urgent Primary Care

Cwm Taf Morgannwg (CTM) University Health Board

As clinical director for CTM Urgent Primary Care I am responsible for overseeing the day to day clinical management of the service. At present we provide urgent out of hours primary care for over 300,000 people including 80 care homes. Below is an overview of my current responsibilities.

September 2020 - present
Governance and Performance:

I am responsible for managing the clinical workforce, addressing performance issues, as well as investigating and responding to critical incidents and complaints. I am consistently praised for my report writing skills, particulraly when it comes to legal statements and incident reports. My duties also extend to chairing the urgent primary care governance panel.

Strategy:

During the COVID-19 pandemic, I have been responsible for ensuring that our infection prevention and control strategies are inline with national guidance. I have introduced criteria for red and amber streams within the primary care centres, and worked with infection control colleagues to ensure that appropriate PPE strategies are employed within the department. I have also introduced a “limited examination” strategy based on the literature published by the Centre for Evidence Based Medicine, to provide a safe mechanism of collecting vital signs for remote clinicians.

GP training:

I oversee the training of GP registrars within the out of hours service and am responsible for trainee inductions. I regularly provide supervision for GPST2 and 3 on shift.

GP recruitment:

I am proactive in recruitment of GPs to the service and provide regular inductions for new starters. I am involved in workforce planning and am responsible for composing clinical job descriptions.

Projects during this post

Introduction of 111:

This is an example of my experience in "Change Management"

  • I lead the recent integration of CTM UPC with 111 from a clinical stand point. This involved frequent discussions with both the local health board and Welsh ambulance service (WAST).
  • I was responsible for presenting at executive boards, community health council and wider stakeholder boards. This relies on the ability to communicate with managerial and clinical boards as well as lay committees.
  • Our management team was praised for our smooth rollout of 111.

Merger of two urgent primary care departments:

This is an example of my experience in "Organisational Change"

  • The recent amalgamation of two healthboards, required the transfer of urgent primare care services from one health board to the other.
  • To enable a succesfull tranfser, I was required meet with stakeholders affected by the change as well as liaiasing with the clinicians.
  • Following a succesful transfer, My main focus now is to manage issues raised due to the new ways of working and working staff to develop solutions.

Clinical lead for CTM 111-First:

This is an example of my experience in "Project Management"
In 2020 the Welsh Government have launched a national initiative to introduce a phone first model for unscheduled care. I was approached by the head of primary care and assistant medical director to lead the project within CTM. The model that we have developed is now being adopted as the national phone first model for Wales.
As well as developing the service model, my responsibilities include:

  • Representing CTM on the national phone first group.
  • Clinical lead for project board. Responsible for clinical and data modelling components of project initiation document (PID) and business case presentation to the executive board.
  • Providing regular updates to the CTM executive board and Welsh Government.
  • Presenting the proposed contact first model to the CHC and wider stakeholders.
  • Liaising with 3rd party IT companies such as One Advanced and INPS.
  • Ensure that there is a robust governance structure across the model.
  • Chair weekly clinical workstream meetings.
